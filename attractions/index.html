Tourist Attractions in India

Ajanta and Ellora

Maharashtra is home to a number of Buddhist caves that not only illustrate the quintessence of cave architecture but also reveal a captivating history. Their finest examples are Ajanta and Ellora caves which are found in Aurangabad city of the state.

Amarnath

India is long known to be the land of magic and mysteries that have enthralled people with miraculous incidences since time immemorial. Amarnath is one such miracle which is believed to be the earthly abode of Lord Shiva.

Bharatpur

Bharatpur is the place for India’s most spectacular display of wildlife with a dash of awe-inspiring historical remnants. Around 178 km distance from the Rajasthan's capital, Jaipur will bring you to this dusty city. With history having its traces in 5th century BC, the city was nurtured by Matsya dynasty followed by Maharaja Suraj Mal.

Bodhgaya

Bodhgaya is one of the four major pilgrimage sites of Buddhists located in the Bihar state of India. Other sites are Kushinagar, Lumbini and Sarnath. The place holds religious importance for being the place where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment in Mahabodhi complex, under a banyan tree which later became famous as Bodhi Tree.

Char Dham Yatra

India shares an inextricable bond with religions that have always been a centre of fascination for the world. The galore of annual festivals, pilgrimages and harvest celebrations keep alive the spirit of ethos and traditions. Of all the religious events, one of the most revered is Char Dham Yatra.

Jim Corbett National Park

Dive in and marvel over the exotic wildlife at the first national park of India, Jim Corbett National park. It was established in 1936 by the notable hunter and naturalist, Jim Corbett as Hailey National Park.

Elephanta Caves

Slip into the thought-provoking monument, Elephanta caves in Mumbai that present an unmatchable rock-cut architectural beauty from the yesteryears. It is located in an offshore Elephanta Island which lies 10 km away from Mumbai.

Golden Temple

Golden Temple is the holiest pilgrimage site of Sikhs that draws devotees and travelers alike with its serenity and lustrous beauty. Also known as Harmandir Sahib and Darbar Sahib, it is sited in Amritsar city of Punjab.

Hampi

Journey to India's one of the most enthralling historic ruins, Hampi is a whole new experience in itself. Situated in Karnataka, it used to serve the famous Vijayanagar Empire as its capital city in the 14th century.

Khajuraho

Seductively beautiful and immaculately embellished, Khajuraho has been luring passerby since the time of legends. It is a Group of Monuments binding together 25 Hindu and Jain temples so powerful that makes your head swim.

Khandala

Konark Sun Temple

Konark Sun temple is the epitome of Odisha style of temple architecture or Kalinga architecture marking the zenith of Eastern Ganga dynasty. King Narasimha Deva I built this temple in the 12th Century AD.

Lake Palace

No place in India exemplifies opulence and royalty better than the Lake Palace in Udaipur. Earlier called Jagniwas, this classic gem used to be favorite retreat from the summer heat of the royals of Mewar.

Meenakshi Temple

Meenakshi temple culminates the glory of South Indian temple architecture illustrating a fantastic spectacle of history. It extends its boundaries in an area of a whopping 65000 sq. m. for which people nicknamed it 'a City of its own'. The beautiful location on the bank of Vaigai River multiplies its beauty.

Mysore Palace

Mysore, the celebrated city of Karnataka is known by many names like 'Cultural capital of Karnataka' and 'City of Palaces'. Heritage structures and convivial festivities make Mysore one of the most visited cities in South India.

Ranthambore

Ranthambore is mainly known for Ranthambore National Park, one of the most enthralling wildlife areas in India to spot the famous Bengal Tigers. It is named after the historic Ranthambore Fort which makes the place more enchanting.

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi Stupa is a historically significant Buddhist monument located in Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh. The main highlight of this dusty town is enormous hemispherical structure called Stupa which houses relics of Buddha.

Shirdi

Shirdi is one of the most sacred temples in India, the abode of the famous saint Shri Satchidanand Sadguru Sainath Maharaj, fondly known as ‘Saibaba’. Streams of devout that follow him vehemently flock to the temple all year round.

Taj Mahal

Umaid Bhawan Palace

Umaid Bhawan Palace is one of the largest residential palaces belonging to the royals of Rajasthan. The present owner of the palace is Gaj Raj Singh, a descendant of Maharaja Umaid Singh. It is sited on Chittar hill on the outskirts of Jodhpur city.
