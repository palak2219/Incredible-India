Srinagar

A gorgeous blend of the breathtaking panorama, romantic Shikara rides, and delicious Wazwan cuisine- Srinagar captures the essence of the beautiful Kashmir Valley. Incredibly romantic and irresistibly spectacular, this spectacular city is one of the most famous honeymoon destinations on the global travel map. Join us for a sojourn in paradise on our Srinagar tour as we explore this awe-inspiring valley of beauty.

Alluring Srinagar Attractions of Srinagar

Dal Lake

The poster child of Kashmir Tourism Dal Lake is a vast clear sheet of water mirroring the looming Pir Panjal peaks. The best way to experience the charms of Dal is to stay in a houseboat on the lake. Built traditionally with all modern amenities, these colorful houseboats exude the opulence of the lost royal era. Go for a Shikara ride on the lake, visit the beautiful Char Chinari Island, buy some flowers at the floating market, and soak in the idyllic ambiance of Dal.

The Mughal Gardens

Established during the Mughal era, and blossoming through the ages, these royal gardens are fine specimens of artistic landscaping and elaborate architecture blended with natural splendor. The three gardens- Shalimar, Nishat, and Chashm-e-Shahi, set on the banks of Jhelum with snowy peaks in the backdrop, are a shutterbug’s delight.

Shankaracharya Temple

This 11th century Shiva temple stands at the top of Shankaracharya Hill that is regarded as the oldest site in Srinagar, dating back to 250 BC. The wooded hill offers you breathtaking views of the valley sprawled at its feet.

Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden

Spread over an expanse of over 26 acres, this is the largest tulip garden in Asia. Set by the glittering waters of Dal with Zarbawan range in the backdrop, this beautiful garden is an endless rainbow of gorgeous tulips. Its large variety of tulips certainly gives the gardens of the Netherlands a run for their money.

Food in Srinagar

The delicious Kashmiri platter called Wazwan is a delight for non-vegetarians. Mouthwatering Rista-rice, Rogan Josh, kebabs, Goshtba, and Yakhni Pulav are sure to delight your taste buds & tummies. Vegetarians would love the delicious Dum Alu, sweetened Modur Pulav, and creamy cottage cheese recipes. Eat your curries with the local bread Khambir and down it all with a cup of fragrant Kahwa.

Best Time to Visit

Srinagar is a charming destination in all seasons. Rainbow spring, lush summers, golden-orange falls, and pristine-white winters- take your pick! However, the peak tourist season here is from March to October

How to Reach

By Air – Srinagar Airport connects the valley to major Indian cities.

By Rail – Jammu Tawi Railway Station, around 275 km away, is the nearest railhead connecting Srinagar to the rest of the country.

By Road – Srinagar is well-connected to major towns in the region by a network of roads & highways.
