Hyderabad

A quintessential metropolitan with a glorious history, rich heritage, and old-world charm, Hyderabad welcomes you to the realm of the Nizams. While the upcoming areas are dotted with swanky malls & IT parks, its charms lie in the rustic alleyways of the old city. Home to many heritage monuments & museums, unmatchable Hyderabadi Biryani, and gorgeous pearl jewelry, Hyderabad promises you to soak you in its culture, cuisine, and colorful ambiance.

Alluring Attractions of Hyderabad

Char Minar

Char Minar, Hyderabad’s most photographed monument, is synonymous with the city. Located in the old Hyderabad area, the structure was named so for the tall fluted minarets on its four corners. A mosque is located on its top floor. It was built by Mohammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1591 to commemorate the founding of the city of Hyderabad. Over the centuries, Char Minar has stood the test of time and is still an iconic landmark of Hyderabad.

Golconda Fort

Built on a granite hill on the outskirts of Hyderabad, Golconda Fort is an enamoring blend of strength and grandeur. A humble mud fort of Kakatiya rulers in the 12th century, Golconda was fortified by the Qutb Shahi rulers into a granite wonder. An architectural & acoustical genius of its time, Golconda fort is now a ravaged jumble of structure within its tall ramparts that bear testimony to its bygone glory. The Light & Sound Show held at the fort depicts its glorious history and secrets hidden its many nooks & crannies.

Salar Jung Musuem

One of the largest private collections in the world, Salar Jung Museum is a treasure trove of world-class art & artifacts. This priceless collection by Mir Yousef Ali Khan showcases a variety of stone sculptures, bronze images, painted textiles, wooden carvings, miniature paintings, modern art, ivory carvings, jade carvings, metal-ware, manuscripts, and arms & armor from across the globe.

Qutb Shahi Tombs

Built on an elevated base in grey granite with exquisite ornamentation, Qutb Shahi Tombs are home to the mausoleums. A perfect blend of Indo-Persian architecture, these tombs depict the end of an era in Hyderabad.

Mecca Mosque

An imposing structure in granite, Mecca Mosque is one of the oldest mosques in India. Commissioned by Quli Qutb Shah but completed by Aurangazeb after he annexed the Golconda Kingdom, this spectacular mosque is an invaluable heritage of Hyderabad.

Food in Hyderabad

Hyderabad and Dum Biryani go hand in hand! But there is more to please your palate. This Nizami city offers delicious non-vegetarian delicacies like Haleem, Boti ka salan, Keema Samosa, and Paaya-Naan to name a few. Tea enthusiasts must try the Irani tea with Osmania biscuits. Shahi Tukda and Khubani ka Meetha are two delectable sweet offerings of Hyderabadi cuisine.
