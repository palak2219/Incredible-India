Tirupati

The end of a spiritual quest for some and awakening of spirituality for others, Tirupati is the gateway to the holy temple of Lord Balaji on the hills of Tirumala. This little temple town in the Chittoor district of Andhra is dotted with many temples, most of them devoted to Lord Vishnu in different forms, but the ultimate honor goes to the Tirupati Balaji Temple on the seventh hill of Tirumala. A pilgrimage, a scenic spot, and a soul seeker’s refuge, Tirupati embraces all those who enter the realm of Lord Balaji.

Alluring Attractions of Tirupati

Tirupati Balaji Temple

This huge temple complex, spread in an area of over 2.2 acres, is one of the most visited & richest pilgrimages across the globe. The temple crests the seventh hill on the Tirumala range and can be easily accessed on foot or by a vehicle. Its magnificent architecture, highlighted by gold-covered doors, pillars & domes, is quite exemplary. The presiding deity, Lord Venkateswar, fondly called Balaji, appears here in the form of an 8 ft tall black idol, dressed in elegant clothing & glittering jewels, and accompanied by his consorts Goddesses Srilakshmi & Padmavati. The idol's eyes are covered, supporting the premise that his intense gaze would burn the universe. This temple, steeped deep in legends & lore, casts a spiritual spell on the devotees standing in serpentine queues to receive Lord Balaji’s blessings.

Sri Kapileswara Swami Temple

Located at the foot of the hills, this Shiva temple is visited by devotees before going on to Lord Balaji’s temple on Tirumala. The devotees bathe in the holy waters of Kapil Teertham to rid of all their sins and purify their souls before they embark on their journey to the realm of Tirupati Balaji.

Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swami Temple

According to a legend, Lord Balaji and Goddess Padmavathy stayed here for six months after their wedding before moving up to their abode in Tirumala Hills. Thus, newlyweds come to this temple to offer their first prayers for a long & happy wedded life.

Sri Govindaraja Swami Temple

One of the largest & oldest temples in Tirupati, Govindraja Temple is easily recognizable by its tall gopuram that dominates the skyline of this temple town. Though the temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu as Govindraja, it has many smaller shrines devoted to other gods & goddesses within the complex.

Tirupati Prasadam

Tirupati Laddos and savory Vadas are offered to the Lord and then sold & distributed amidst the devotees as Balaji’s Prasadam or blessings. Prepared in special kitchens, with specially sourced ingredients, the dry fruit laddoos & Vadas are quite delicious. Also do try the delicious Upma prasadam here that is offered free to the devotees.

Best time to Visit

Lord Balaji’s devotees throng the temple throughout the year to receive his blessings. However, the best time to visit the temple would be in winter from September to March when the weather is pleasant

How to reach

By Air : Tirupati International Airport, Renigunta, around 15 km away, connects the temple town to the major cities in India.

By Rail : Tirupati Railway Station connects the temple town to the major cities in south India. By Road : A well-maintained network of roads & highways connects Tirupati with cities in the region.
